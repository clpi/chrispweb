Richard Feynman

The man, the myth, the legend

Short bio

Perhaps beyond his monumentous achievement achievements within the fields of quantum mechanics and particle physics, Richard Feynman is best remembered as a natural born teacher. His distinctive New York accent and the lovable smile he carried while breaking down difficult concepts into decidedly more digestible topics are still teaching their lessons to this day.

Timeline

  • 1918: Born in New York City
  • 1932: Received BS from MIT, named Putnam fellow
  • 1942: Receives PhD from Princeton
  • 1942-1945: Worked on Manhattan project
  • 1945: Taught theoretical physics at Cornell
  • 1950: Began research at Caltech
  • 1965: Received Nobel Prize in physics for work in Quantum Electrodynamics with Julian Schwinger and Shinichiro Tomonaga
  • 1979: Received National Medal of Science award
